How Billology works

Four steps from a paper bill to answers about your spending. Figures always come from the bill itself — the AI only reads and explains, it never invents a number.

1
Add a bill
Take a photo, choose an image or PDF, or paste the bill's text using the buttons at the top.
2
Check the extraction
Billology reads the merchant, date, items, taxes, and total, and runs arithmetic checks. Anything that doesn't add up is flagged with the conflicting figures as evidence.
3
Review & save
Pick a category and save. If the date is missing you'll be asked to set it. Saved bills become part of your history.
4
Browse your bills
"My bills" lists everything you saved — search by merchant, filter by category, delete what you don't need.
5
See your spending
The Spending panel shows this month's total, category breakdown, monthly trend, and top merchants — all computed from your saved bills.
6
Ask questions
Ask things like "How much did I spend on groceries in May?" — answered from your saved records only. If the data isn't there, it says so instead of guessing.
!
Privacy & AI
Bill images are read by a cloud AI by default. The provider menu lets you use your own key or a fully local model (Ollama). The moon/sun button switches dark and light themes.
